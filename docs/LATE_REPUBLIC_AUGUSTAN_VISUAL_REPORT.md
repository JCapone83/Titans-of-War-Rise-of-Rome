# Late-Republic and Augustan Visual Report

## Accepted preproduction batch

This batch establishes five major-precinct assets for the transition from the late Republic into the Augustan capital:

- `public/images/projects/theatre-of-pompey-v1.png` — Text-and-evidence synthesis
- `public/images/projects/forum-of-augustus-v1.png` — Evidence-led reconstruction
- `public/images/projects/mausoleum-of-augustus-v1.png` — Text-and-evidence synthesis
- `public/images/projects/theatre-of-marcellus-v1.png` — Evidence-led reconstruction
- `public/images/projects/agrippan-pantheon-v1.png` — Uncertainty-labeled reconstruction hypothesis

All five files use the existing three-quarter orthographic camera, warm neutral light, mixed Roman materials, transparent alpha, and complete-site framing. They are large project-surface illustrations intended to remain legible at approximately 160 pixels rather than district-map icons.

## Chronology and rejection rules

The Theatre of Pompey is a late-Republic precinct: a semicircular theater crowned by the Temple of Venus Victrix, with the stage, portico garden, and curia relationship visible. It must never be read as the Colosseum or as an Augustan foundation.

The Forum of Augustus is shown at its 2 BC dedication. The Temple of Mars Ultor, curved exedrae, statue program, constrained site, and high Subura fire wall are essential. It must not appear as an isolated temple in parkland.

The Mausoleum of Augustus begins in 28 BC. Its broad circular base is evidence-led, while the planted upper profile remains debated. The cutaway exposes concentric rings for gameplay legibility; it does not claim an open monument.

The Theatre of Marcellus is a semicircular theater with two dominant exterior arcade levels and a restrained upper awning structure. It must not acquire the full elliptical plan or repeated monumental tiers of the Flavian Amphitheatre.

The Agrippan Pantheon is a conservative late-first-century-BC sacred precinct. The surviving Hadrianic rotunda, concrete dome, coffered interior, and oculus are prohibited. The accepted asset uses a columned front, rectangular roofed hall, open court, drainage, and modest Campus Martius context to make that chronological distinction visible.

## Runtime boundary

These assets are approved visual inputs, not an implemented Act X. They must not be added to a runtime mapping until the corresponding project definitions, dates, evidence labels, construction stages, maintenance burdens, and deterministic tests exist. Theatre of Pompey may instead be used on a late-Republic historical-context surface after that surface has a stable implementation contract.

## Current runtime use

Theatre of Pompey alone is now used on the Ides historical-context surface. The Forum of Augustus, Mausoleum of Augustus, Theatre of Marcellus, and Agrippan Pantheon remain preproduction assets for a future Act X implementation.

## Production record

The assets were generated with the built-in OpenAI image-generation tool under project-authored prompts on July 19, 2026. Flat chroma backgrounds were removed locally with the standard Codex chroma-key helper. Each final file is a 1536 by 1024 RGBA PNG with transparent corners. The first Theatre of Marcellus pass was rejected because its facade read too strongly as the Colosseum; the accepted revision reduces the exterior to two dominant arcaded levels and clarifies the half-round theater plan.
