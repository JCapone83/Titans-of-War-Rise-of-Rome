# Act XII Visual Production Report

## Scope and boundary

This batch completes the six player-facing capital works in Act XII, AD 96-117. The images are historically informed game reconstructions, not archaeological plates. Their purpose is to make each operating system legible at card scale while preserving the game's distinction between secure physical evidence, plausible reconstruction, and functional abstraction.

The standing Hadrianic Pantheon is outside the campaign's chronology and is absent from this set. All six assets use the established three-quarter isometric camera, warm upper-left daylight, restrained stone and terracotta palette, complete silhouette, and transparent 1536 x 1024 presentation.

## Forum of Trajan

**File:** `public/images/projects/forum-trajan-v1.png`

**Evidence status:** Evidence-led integrated complex with reconstructed upper architecture.

**Visual concept:** The image treats the forum as one excavated and retained administrative precinct rather than a freestanding temple card. A broad paved court and flanking porticoes lead to the Basilica Ulpia. Semicircular exedrae and retaining structures explain the scale of the Quirinal intervention. Two library ranges frame one and only one Trajan's Column in the rear court. The surrounding service edge prevents the monument from appearing detached from the city that supplied and staffed it.

**Materials and game read:** Pale cut stone and marble establish official ceremony; terracotta roofs, service structures, and circulation edges keep the complex operational. The court, basilica, libraries, column, and excavation boundary remain distinct when reduced to the project card.

**Reconstruction limit:** The broad program and surviving remains are more secure than every elevation, roof treatment, statue, internal arrangement, and construction phase. The image therefore carries an integrated-complex label rather than claiming exact restoration.

**Reject if revised:** Reject duplicate monumental columns, a modern isolated-column plaza, a generic Greek sanctuary, a domed Pantheon, missing Quirinal retention, or an image in which the Basilica Ulpia and libraries cannot be distinguished.

## Trajanic Administrative Complex

**File:** `public/images/projects/trajan-administrative-complex-v1.png`

**Evidence status:** Evidence-led office and archive synthesis.

**Visual concept:** A low civil compound combines a central hearing hall, stepped audience seating, archives, clerical rooms, controlled courts, messenger circulation, and service spaces. The entrances remain restrained and continuous with the office ranges. The design deliberately avoids presenting the complex as a fortress or assigning the uncertain rooms a single modern bureaucratic name.

**Materials and game read:** Brick-faced concrete, plastered walls, stone thresholds, timber shelving, and terracotta roofs distinguish ordinary administration from the marble spectacle of the forum. Open courts and visible archive rooms communicate record keeping and human movement.

**Reconstruction limit:** Administrative use is represented as a functional synthesis. Exact room ownership, archive distribution, upper stories, and the relationship among later phases are not presented as settled.

**Reject if revised:** Reject crenellations, oversized guard towers, a canal or harbor, a palace throne room, modern filing systems, or a single decorative market hall with no visible archives and service circulation.

## Baths of Trajan and Sette Sale

**File:** `public/images/projects/baths-trajan-v1.png`

**Evidence status:** Evidence-led bath precinct and hydraulic synthesis.

**Visual concept:** The baths read as a large symmetrical public establishment laid across the earlier Domus Aurea zone. Central bathing halls, palaestra courts, gardens, circulation ranges, furnace and fuel service, storage, drains, and staff access make dedication only the start of an operating burden. A separate but connected multi-chamber Sette Sale cistern block makes the water support visible instead of hiding it behind the facade.

**Materials and game read:** Brick-faced concrete and tiled roofs carry the working mass; selective marble and pools mark public rooms. The detached cistern silhouette and repeated chambers remain legible at card scale.

**Reconstruction limit:** The relationship of surviving fabric, service routes, water connections, and individual phases is partly reconstructed. The asset shows the system required to operate the baths without asserting that every connection is archaeologically fixed.

**Reject if revised:** Reject a single domed spa, a Christian basilica, a late-antique ruin, exposed modern plumbing, a palace without service yards, or an image where the Sette Sale support cannot be recognized.

## Aqua Traiana

**File:** `public/images/projects/aqua-traiana-v1.png`

**Evidence status:** Evidence-led distributed water-system synthesis.

**Visual concept:** The image begins with multiple spring-capture houses on a wooded ridge. Covered gravity conduits follow the terrain through settling basins and inspection points to a principal reservoir. A short arcade crosses only the shallow valley that requires it; branches then distribute water toward serviced districts. Repair paths and crews make maintenance access part of the architecture.

**Materials and game read:** Stone capture houses, lined channels, masonry access points, water surfaces, paths, and the compact reservoir create a clear source-to-distribution sequence. The cutaway presentation exposes selected conduit runs for instruction, even though most working channels would be covered.

**Reconstruction limit:** Source locations, route details, later repairs, and local distribution arrangements are not wholly recoverable. The image compresses a regional network into a single readable terrain model and does not claim literal scale or adjacency.

**Reject if revised:** Reject another continuous Aqua Claudia arcade, a modern dam, metal pipelines, pumps, fantasy machinery, a decorative fountain with no source system, or exposed channels presented as the normal form of the whole conduit.

## Trajanic Portus

**File:** `public/images/projects/trajanic-portus-v1.png`

**Evidence status:** Evidence-led dual-harbor and supply-system synthesis.

**Visual concept:** The broad Claudian sea harbor, moles, and lighthouse remain in service in the foreground. Behind them, Trajan's unmistakable hexagonal inner basin provides protected working water. Canals join the basins and continue toward the Tiber transfer system. Horrea, quays, cranes, inspection points, small transfer craft, roads, towpaths, drainage, and flood banks make the port a grain and logistics machine rather than a scenic waterfront.

**Materials and game read:** Blue water separates the outer and inner systems; pale moles and quays define the harbor geometry; long terracotta-roofed warehouses establish storage capacity. The hexagon remains visible at reduced size.

**Reconstruction limit:** The retained Claudian harbor and Trajanic basin are secure at the system level, while exact warehouse phases, transfer channels, flood works, craft placement, and operating sequence remain partly reconstructed.

**Reject if revised:** Reject a modern container terminal, late or medieval towers, giant fantasy ships, loss of the hexagonal basin, removal of the Claudian outer harbor, or a basin isolated from warehouses and transfer routes.

## Trajanic Circus and inherited valley

**File:** `public/images/projects/trajanic-circus-v1.png`

**Evidence status:** Evidence-led valley and circulation synthesis.

**Visual concept:** A long U-ended racing arena is enclosed by tiered seating and Roman substructures. Starting gates, the central spina, restrained imperial monuments, entrances, crowd routes, water and sanitation points, service courts, and the developed valley edge make the Circus Maximus a managed urban precinct. The image presents Trajanic completion as another layer in a long inherited site rather than a new foundation.

**Materials and game read:** The pale track and seating form the dominant silhouette. Terracotta service roofs, planted edges, pools, and access courts show the support system without obscuring the racecourse.

**Reuse boundary:** `Titans_of_Constantinople_Ashes_of_Nika/public/assets/constantinople-532-map.png` was consulted only as a project-owned composition reference for the long Hippodrome track and circulation geometry. No Byzantine wall, palace, church, obelisk, urban fabric, or source pixel was carried into this newly generated Roman asset. The existing Flavian Amphitheatre asset supplied the Roman project's camera and palette conventions.

**Reconstruction limit:** The broad topography and long development of the site are more secure than every AD 117 elevation, service building, monument, and dedication phase. The compressed precinct is an evidence-led synthesis rather than an exact site plan.

**Reject if revised:** Reject Constantinopolitan walls or churches, medieval fabric, a circular amphitheatre, an empty track without starting gates and support routes, excessive late monuments, a cropped long axis, or an isolated stadium detached from its valley precinct.

## Production and review record

The six images were generated with the built-in OpenAI image-generation tool under project-authored prompts on July 20, 2026. Aqua Claudia, Claudian Portus, and the Flavian Amphitheatre were used only as original-project style and continuity references. Chroma-key masters were converted to transparent RGBA PNGs, normalized to 1536 x 1024, and reviewed together on a dark contact sheet for silhouette, edge quality, color continuity, and card-scale distinction.

Acceptance requires all six files, exact project-to-art mappings, descriptive alternative text, visible evidence labels, transparent corners, valid alpha, passing simulation and balance tests, a successful production build, and browser review at desktop and mobile widths.
