# Act IX Balance Report

## Scope

Act IX covers **49-27 BC**. It begins only after the player accepts an explicit same-year bridge from the Act VIII judgment at 49 BC. Six playable councils address Caesar's emergency command, repair of the civic core, the succession crisis after the Ides, triumviral war finance, Actium and demobilization, and the operating settlement of 27 BC.

The act ends at 27 BC. It does not pretend that the constitutional arrangements of that year created the finished Principate in one stroke, and it does not begin a separate Augustan building expansion.

## Mechanical Claim

The act keeps command, institutions, demobilization, property, finance, public provision, succession, and monumental credit in separate ledgers. Winning command does not by itself settle veteran claims, restore courts, preserve archives, or provide a durable succession.

Four staged works convert political choices into operating obligations:

- Forum of Caesar and Temple of Venus Genetrix
- Curia Julia
- Basilica Julia
- Veteran Land and Road Registry

The Curia requires an operating Forum of Caesar precinct. Every completed work adds recurring upkeep, and inherited works from earlier acts remain obligations rather than free bonuses.

## Historical Boundaries

Participant testimony is treated as interested evidence. Cicero, Caesar, Augustus, and the authors of public honors had political purposes; Appian, Plutarch, Suetonius, and Cassius Dio wrote later and knew the outcome. Coins, inscriptions, fasti, offices, provincial assignments, archaeology, and legal practice constrain the literary narratives.

The 27 BC council therefore grades an operating settlement, not a modern written constitution. It allows a concentrated Augustan form, a negotiated republican restoration, or a collegial military compact while exposing the costs required to keep each one working.
