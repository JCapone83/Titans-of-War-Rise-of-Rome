# Act X Capital Integration Report

## Augustan Capital View

The city map now gains an optional Augustan Capital View during Act X. It uses the eight approved project images to show unlocked works at schematic positions associated with the Palatine, Roman Forum and Subura, Circus Flaminius, Campus Martius, and urban wards. These positions support strategy play; they are explicitly not an archaeological survey or a claim that the compact six-district game map reproduces ancient street coordinates.

Reserved sites, foundations and service work, structural shells, and operating institutions have different visual treatments. The map therefore records construction rather than presenting every Augustan dedication as complete. Agrippa's Pantheon remains an uncertainty-labeled pre-Hadrianic predecessor and is not represented as the surviving domed rotunda.

## Operating systems

Four summaries translate the detailed Augustan ledger into readable capital systems:

- **Civic government:** Senate and magistrates, administration, public access, provincial review, and concentrated patronage.
- **Public provision:** annona reliability, access, maintenance, and urban administration.
- **Urban safety:** fire coverage, maintenance, and ward administration.
- **Succession and memory:** succession confidence, household and Senate balance, public memory, and command recognition.

Every score is a bounded derivative of visible Act X measures. No second resource economy or hidden success condition was added. The original twelve measures and three forecast gaps remain authoritative and visible.

## AD 14 endpoint

The campaign ending and exported chronicle now record the four operating systems and the number of landmark works operating or still active. This makes the final judgment about a functioning capital, not simply a monument count.

**No Act XI** is introduced. AD 14 remains the last turn, the save schema remains version 14, and all project costs, council effects, reference strategies, and historical evidence labels are unchanged.
