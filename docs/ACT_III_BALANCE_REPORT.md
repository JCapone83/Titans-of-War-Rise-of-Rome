# Act III Balance Report

Act III extends the same inherited city through six republican turns, from 509 to the traditional Gallic crisis date of 390 BC. Each reference strategy begins from a different tested Acts I-II city. The balance runner resolves all eleven campaign councils, records the Veii settlement and Gallic posture, rejects skipped actions, and requires an overall score of at least 70.

## Paid Siege and Citizen Settlement

- Overall: **82**
- Veii settlement: land distribution
- Gallic posture: prepared, 80
- Claim: public accounts and soldier pay can sustain a long campaign while land rewards preserve citizen support, provided the inherited city has food and household depth.

## Rotating Levy and Tributary Veii

- Overall: **87**
- Veii settlement: tributary incorporation
- Gallic posture: prepared, 70
- Claim: a strong commercial inheritance can protect campaign seasons, preserve Veientine production, and exchange treasury for time without requiring concentrated command.

## Command and Field Army

- Overall: **74**
- Veii settlement: ritual destruction
- Gallic posture: exposed, 47
- Claim: concentrated authority can produce formidable tactical readiness and break Veii, but coercion, debt, and brittle consent leave a field army without strategic depth. The route remains viable and scores well militarily; it is deliberately weak in civic balance.

## Regression Boundary

The Acts I-II reference scores remain **79, 80, 92, and 77**. Act III therefore adds new state and scoring consequences without retuning the established opening game. Run `npm run balance` to reproduce all seven campaigns.

The traditional ten-year siege, Camillan narrative, soldier-pay chronology, and details of the Gallic crisis remain uncertain. The simulation treats these as a framework for fiscal, household, and strategic pressures rather than as a literal annual reconstruction.
