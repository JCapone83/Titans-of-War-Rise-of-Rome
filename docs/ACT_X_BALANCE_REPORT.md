# Act X Balance Report: The Augustan City

## Scope

Act X spans the 27 BC-AD 14 Augustan settlement as an explicit continuation from the completed civil-war act. It covers seven playable dates from 23 BC through AD 14 and ends with the first transfer after Augustus. It does not treat the constitutional language of restoration, the ruling household, or monumental construction as substitutes for operating capacity.

The act tracks twelve independent measures: princeps authority, Senate and magistrate capacity, household standing, succession confidence, urban administration, fire coverage, annona reliability, monumental memory, patronage concentration, provincial command balance, public access, and maintenance capacity.

## Historical Guardrails

- The arrangements of 27 and 23 BC are successive operating settlements, not one finished written constitution.
- The Agrippan Pantheon is an uncertainty-labeled reconstruction hypothesis. It is not the Hadrianic domed rotunda, and the asset and text must never borrow that later building's coffered dome or oculus.
- The Ara Pacis joins senatorial ritual and the language of peace to provincial command rather than presenting peace as costless.
- The Forum of Augustus combines courts, military ceremony, religion, memory, and household credit.
- The vigiles ward network is a game abstraction of the AD 6 service, not a claim that one surviving station supplies the whole plan.
- The AD 14 ending scores succession across household, Senate, offices, armies, provinces, and urban operation rather than treating inheritance alone as sufficient.

## Balance Rules

No one choice is universally dominant. Central command improves coordination but can weaken public channels. Civic dispersion preserves magistrates and access but can slow response. Household concentration improves recognition and speed but raises patronage and succession risks. Monumental memory contributes to the score only alongside public access and maintenance. Fire and grain services carry recurring costs.

The project portfolios are intentionally incomplete at AD 14. A playable stage represents a bounded historical interval, and an operating project creates upkeep rather than becoming a permanent free bonus.
