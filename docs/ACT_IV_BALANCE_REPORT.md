# Act IV Balance Report

Act IV resolves the Gallic crisis from the state earned in Act III, then carries persistent damage through emergency relief, reconstruction, debt and land claims, and the Latin settlement of 338 BC. Prepared cities still suffer nonzero damage. Exposed cities retain surviving districts and institutions, but they face a deliberately severe recovery.

## Rapid Household Return

- Overall: **78**
- Crisis inherited: prepared
- Reconstruction: rapid
- Latin settlement: reciprocal league
- Claim: stores and household depth can return people to familiar plots quickly, but fire exposure and weak circulation remain real costs.

## Surveyed Civic Recovery

- Overall: **87**
- Crisis inherited: prepared
- Reconstruction: planned
- Latin settlement: differentiated compacts
- Claim: a commercial and institutional inheritance can absorb slower, more expensive surveys and emerge with stronger records and regional flexibility.

## Fortified Roman Recovery

- Overall: **74**
- Crisis inherited: strained
- Reconstruction: militarized
- Latin settlement: Roman command
- Claim: concentrated authority combined with defense in depth can survive a costly crisis and restore military power, but civic balance remains extremely weak. The exposed field-battle variant remains playable as a challenge route and is not mislabeled as a balanced recovery.

## Regression Boundary

The published Acts I-II scores remain **79, 80, 92, and 77**. The Act III scores remain **82, 87, and 74**. Run `npm run balance` to reproduce all ten campaigns and fail the command if a reference route drops below 70 or skips a declared action.

Literary accounts of the sack, Capitoline defense, ransom, Camillus, and hurried rebuilding are not treated as a modern eyewitness record. District damage is a deterministic strategic model grounded in readiness, reserves, defenses, institutions, and exposure.
