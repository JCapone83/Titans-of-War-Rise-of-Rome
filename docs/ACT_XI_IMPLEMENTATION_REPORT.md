# Act XI Implementation Report

## Scope

Act XI, **Imperial Capital**, extends the playable campaign from AD 14-96. It adds nine dated turns and councils, ending with the succession after Domitian rather than treating imperial government as a single uninterrupted system.

The act is built around a deliberate contrast: Rome approaches its most recognizable monumental form while the political machinery behind that form accumulates guard brokerage, army-recognition problems, palace concentration, maintenance burdens, fire exposure, and succession risk. These pressures are measured separately. The game does not present later decline as inevitable or claim that one building caused it.

## Playable systems

- Twelve imperial-capital measures distinguish authority, Senate relations, guard influence, army recognition, succession, provision, fire resilience, harbor supply, palace concentration, provincial trust, maintenance, and public access.
- Four derived operating systems summarize authority transfer, capital provision, urban safety, and the public city versus the palace.
- Eight multi-stage projects carry construction costs, recurring burdens, prerequisites, and evidence labels.
- The AD 64 fire is a timed shock. Prior investment changes the damage but cannot remove the event from the chronology.
- The Flavian Amphitheatre opens in AD 80 as a public work before its later permanent substructure is complete. Its route requires conversion of the former palace-lake precinct to public use.
- The AD 96 outcome records a public Flavian, provision-first, military-recognized, or palace-administrative operating form without collapsing every measure into one victory number.

## Chronological boundary

AD 96 is an act boundary, not the campaign's final historical ambition. Act XII is reserved for AD 96-117 and the Trajanic capital: Forum and Markets, baths, water, harbor capacity, military recognition, provincial command, maintenance, and the succession problem at maximum territorial extent.

The present domed Pantheon is not an Act XI or Trajanic completion. Agrippa's earlier Pantheon remains represented in its own Augustan context; the **Hadrianic Pantheon** begins after the planned AD 117 endpoint.

## Visual gate

Act XI uses textual evidence labels and visible project stages but does not yet claim finished reconstruction art. The next visual batch should prioritize the Flavian Amphitheatre, Aqua Claudia, Claudian Portus, the Domus Aurea precinct before and after public conversion, the Temple of Peace, and the Domitianic palace. Each asset must preserve the established camera, scale, materials, date, and evidence-status rules.
