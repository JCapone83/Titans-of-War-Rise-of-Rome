# Acts I-II Balance Report

**Date:** 2026-07-19
**Command:** `npm run balance`
**Scope:** Hill Settlements and City of Kings, turns 1-10

The reference strategies execute through the same public simulation functions used by the interface. They begin with normal stores and population, pay all construction costs, resolve every council, absorb the flood and raid, and complete or pause major projects under the normal seasonal-capacity rules. The balance gate did not change the grading thresholds.

## Strategy Claims

### Household League

Allocates 55% of available people to farming, 25% to public works, and 20% to levy rotation. It favors reciprocal councils, dispersed housing, wells, grain storage, and modest defense. It produces the weakest military grade of the three while retaining strong urban and logistical results.

### River Exchange

Allocates 45% to farming, 30% to public works, and 25% to levy rotation. It builds around the Tiber market, storage, guarded approaches, practical drainage, and craft. Its low Historical Continuity grade records that a viable city need not reproduce the most monumental traditional sequence.

### Royal Works

Allocates 35% to farming, 45% to public works, and 20% to levy rotation. It concentrates authority and crews on drainage, craft, gates, and the Cloaca Works. It performs best in this ten-turn slice, but commits more households to public obligations and depends on completing its projects without prolonged interruption.

### Crowded Hills Recovery

Begins with 45% of available people on public works and establishes two unserved housing districts, an exposed Tiber market, and fire-using craft before basic networks are ready. From turn three it restores farming, then builds storage, water, Forum drainage, and guarded approaches. Maximum disease exposure falls from 47 after the flawed opening to 22 at the end; maximum fire exposure falls from 60 to 53. The 77 overall result proves that a major planning error is recoverable, while the F in Civic Balance preserves a lasting cost.

## Findings

- All three strategies complete the campaign with an overall score of C or better.
- The separate recovery strategy also completes at C or better without erasing the civic cost of its opening mistake.
- Their action-log signatures and final building sets are distinct.
- No scripted action fails a prerequisite, district rule, material check, or seasonal-capacity check.
- Water, grain, and some drainage remain broadly valuable foundations; the plans differ in when and where they provide them.
- Monumental continuity is not required for a strong overall result: River Exchange scores 84 despite a D in Historical Continuity.

## Known Limitations

- These are deterministic reference plans, not proof that every plausible build order is balanced.
- The current slice has only one major flood and one raid, so repeated-crisis resilience remains untested.
- The Royal Works plan is strongest in this act pair. Later republican constraints should test whether concentrated command remains advantageous.
- Recovery is verified for one crowded-growth failure pattern; military, fiscal, and abandoned-project recovery paths remain untested.
