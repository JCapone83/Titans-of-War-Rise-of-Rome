# Act VI: Mediterranean Republic Balance Report

The optional continuation now runs from the complete 264 BC core judgment through the peace of 201 BC. The 241-218 BC interval is an explicit bridge with a recorded ledger rather than an invisible reset.

## Pressure sequence

- Turns 30-32 introduce fleet capacity, maritime loss, war credit, contractor exposure, provincial trust, imported grain, allied exhaustion, and overseas command duration.
- The interwar bridge applies bounded consequences from fleet doctrine and the Sicilian settlement while preserving the frozen 264 BC judgment and every prior campaign record.
- Turn 33 distinguishes containment, early decisive battle, and compact defense. Seeking the early decision exposes the most emergency reserve and allied endurance.
- Turn 34 prices replacement after Cannae without treating Roman continuation as evidence that the losses were inexpensive.
- Turn 35 separates differentiated settlement, exemplary punishment, and shared recovery across Italian communities.
- Turn 36 settles public credit, veteran claims, allied repair, grain provision, and victory expenditure before the final grade.

## Reference doctrines

The deterministic suite carries three distinct campaigns through all seven Act VI councils:

1. **Distributed Endurance** relies on allied maritime skill, containment, allied-city defense, differentiated settlement, and restored public credit.
2. **State Mobilization** builds public naval capacity, seeks decision, replaces the legions, imposes an exemplary settlement, and grants recorded veteran land.
3. **Bounded Command** limits maritime exposure, defends the compact, ransoms experienced men, funds shared recovery, and pairs victory works with provision.

Each doctrine must finish at turn 36 in 201 BC, resolve every council, cross exactly one chronology bridge, retain ten separate Mediterranean measures, and score at least 60 overall. `scripts/run-balance.mjs` prints final fleet capacity, emergency reserve, provincial trust, allied exhaustion, and veteran settlement pressure so a passing total cannot hide a collapsed component.

## Scope boundary

The act ends in 201 BC. It does not introduce the metropolitan crowding, basilicas, eastern conquest, Gracchan politics, civil wars, or imperial building systems reserved for later acts.
