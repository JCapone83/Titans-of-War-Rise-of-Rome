# Act XI Balance Report

Act XI covers AD 14-96, from the first Augustan transfer through the Flavian and Domitianic capital.

## Verification

The deterministic suite contains 126 passing tests. `npm run balance` completes every published strategy from 753 BC through AD 96 without skipped councils, skipped project actions, dead ends, or results below the Act XI release floor of 60.

## System results

- **Public Flavian Capital:** transfer 91, provision 88, safety 80, public city 94, provincial 93, physical 59.
- **Provision-First Capital:** transfer 85, provision 85, safety 79, public city 94, provincial 95, physical 68.
- **Palace-Administrative Capital:** transfer 64, provision 74, safety 59, public city 33, provincial 65, physical 77.

The palace path is intentionally the narrowest viable route. It gains the strongest physical-project score but records the cost of concentrating urban land and administration around the palace. It remains playable because recorded transfer, provision, published accounts, fire rules, and provincial finance keep the state operating.

## Economy and recurring burden

The Act XI bridge opens an imperial-scale treasury and the seasonal forecast records imperial revenue. This does not make projects free. Inherited works continue to consume upkeep, council decisions consume funds, and completed imperial projects add their own maintenance. Reference routes can therefore sustain one coherent capital program but cannot finish every monument.

## Chronology guardrails

- The AD 64 fire always occurs; preparation alters damage rather than erasing the event.
- The Flavian Amphitheatre requires the public conversion choice in AD 69, opens in AD 80 after three construction stages, and receives its later permanent substructure only after the fourth stage.
- The AD 96 judgment records the completed or active condition of every project.
- No Hadrianic Pantheon, coffered dome, or oculus is introduced before the planned AD 117 endpoint.

## Release judgment

Act XI is mechanically viable and differentiated. Its remaining gate is visual production and browser review, not deterministic balance.
