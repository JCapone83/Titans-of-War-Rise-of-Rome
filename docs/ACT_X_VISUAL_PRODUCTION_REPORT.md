## Visual distinctions

The House of Augustus is an elite domestic compound becoming an official center through linked courts, reception rooms, archives, terraces, and proximity to Apollo. It must not be replaced by the later Domitianic palace.

The Mausoleum cutaway reveals the concentric core for gameplay. The planted upper profile remains a reconstruction and the open wedge is not a claim about its ancient exterior.

The Agrippan Pantheon is a rectangular roofed sacred precinct with a columned front and open court. The standing Hadrianic rotunda, concrete dome, coffered interior, and oculus remain prohibited.

The Baths of Agrippa use an irregular early bath plan with visible water, heat, drainage, fuel, and service infrastructure. They must not become a reduced Baths of Caracalla.

The Theatre of Marcellus has a half-round plan, deep stage building, and two dominant arcade levels. It must not read as the Flavian Amphitheatre.

The Ara Pacis remains a low open-air ritual enclosure in a flood-aware Campus Martius setting. It is not a roofed temple or triumphal arch.

The Forum of Augustus is bounded by its constrained plan, curved exedrae, Temple of Mars Ultor, and high Subura fire wall.

The vigiles station is a functional ward-service compound with water, records, ladders, lamps, rescue tools, and fire equipment. It is not a legionary fort or a modern fire station.
